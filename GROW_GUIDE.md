# GrowTower: The Ultimate Cultivation Guide (Wick System)

This guide provides step-by-step instructions for setting up and managing your GrowTower using the **Passive Wick System**. This method is optimized for the **One-Bud technique** (12/12 light cycle from seed).

---

## 1. Preparation & Materials

### Nutrient & pH Management (Per 800ml Water)
Hydroponic and semi-hydroponic systems require precision. **Always use a pipette for measuring.**
- **Fertilizer:** ~1.8ml **Compo Grow Blüte** (start at the beginning of the flowering phase).
- **pH-Down:** ~2.3ml **Canna pH- Bloom** (Phosphoric Acid). 
    - *Note:* The exact amount depends on your local tap water. Aim for a **pH of 5.8**.
- **Watering:** Always use room-temperature water.

### Growing Medium Options
1.  **Full Hydroponic:** **Pumice (Bimsstein)** as the main medium + **Rockwool (Steinwolle)** for the seed.
2.  **Hybrid Setup:** **Pumice (Bimsstein)** as the main medium + a small amount of **Soil (Erde)** for the seed.

---

## 2. Technical Setup (The Wick System)

Proper wick placement is the "engine" of this system.

1.  **Wick Installation:**
    - Use high-quality nylon or cotton wicks.
    - Place **one wick in each of the four feet** of the Plant Pot.
    - Thread them through the holes so they reach the bottom of the reservoir.
2.  **Braiding the Wicks:**
    - Pull the wicks up into the pot and **braid/weave them upwards** as you fill the pot.
    - This ensures that moisture is transported to the upper layers of the medium.
3.  **Filling with Pumice:**
    - Fill the pot with **Pumice (Bimsstein)** up to about 2-3 cm below the rim.
4.  **The Seed Zone (Top Layer):**
    - **Option A (Rockwool):** Place a pre-soaked Rockwool cube in the center.
    - **Option B (Soil):** Create a small pocket in the center and fill it with high-quality potting soil.
    - **Wick Positioning:** Ensure the ends of your wicks are placed **directly around** the Rockwool or Soil zone to provide immediate moisture to the germinating seed.

---

## 3. The 12/12 One-Bud Timeplan

### Phase 0: Germination
- **Rockwool Preparation:** If using Rockwool, soak it for 24h in pH-adjusted water (pH 5.5) before planting.
- **Sowing:** Plant the seed. Keep the area moist by adding **3ml of water** with a pipette twice daily.
- **Light:** Set your GrowTower timer to a **12/12 cycle** (12h Light / 12h Dark) immediately.

### Week 1-2: Establishment
- **Action:** Continue to keep the seed zone moist using the pipette.
- **Development:** The seedling will stand up and start developing roots. Once the roots hit the wicks/pumice, the system becomes self-sustaining.

### Week 3: Activating the Reservoir
- **Action:** Fill the bottom reservoir with pH-adjusted water (pH 5.8).
- **The Test:** If the water level in the reservoir drops over 48 hours, the capillary action is working. **Stop all top-watering now.**

### Week 4-5: Pre-Flowering & Stretch
- **Action:** Ensure the reservoir never runs dry. The plant will stretch rapidly as it prepares to bloom.
- **Nutrients:** Start adding the nutrient solution (**1.8ml Fertilizer / 800ml water**) as soon as the first white pistils appear.

### Week 6 to Harvest: Flowering Phase
- **Action:** Maintain the reservoir with the nutrient solution.
- **Observation:** If leaves turn very dark green or show "clawing," reduce the fertilizer dosage.

### Final 10-14 Days: Flushing
- **Action:** Stop all fertilizer. Fill the reservoir with **pure, pH-adjusted water only**.
- **Goal:** This forces the plant to use its remaining reserves, resulting in a cleaner and smoother final product.

---

## 4. Critical Rules for Success

1.  **No Top-Watering:** Once the wick system is active, **never water from above.** This prevents flushing accumulated salts from the surface into the sensitive root zone.
2.  **pH Stability:** Especially in the full hydroponic (Pumice) setup, the pH level is critical. Check it regularly to avoid nutrient lockout.
3.  **Pipette Precision:** Do not guess the amounts. Small deviations in fertilizer or pH-down can cause significant stress in an 800ml system.

# GrowTower: The Ultimate Wick-System Guide (One-Bud Method)

This guide provides step-by-step instructions for setting up and managing your GrowTower using a high-performance passive wick system. This method is optimized for the **12/12 One-Bud technique**, skipping the vegetative phase to grow a single, large main flower.

---

## 1. Preparation & Materials

### Nutrient Solution (The "Sweet Spot" Recipe)
Precision is key for hydroponic systems. **Always use a pipette.**
*   **Water Volume:** 800 ml (Standard reservoir refill).
*   **Fertilizer:** **~1.8 ml** Compo Grow Blüte (Mineral). *Do not exceed this at the start of flowering.*
*   **pH Regulation:** **~2.3 ml** Canna pH- Bloom (Phosphoric Acid).
    *   *Note:* The exact amount depends on your local tap water. Aim for a **pH of 5.8 to 6.2**.
*   **Mixing:** Always mix the fertilizer into the water first, then adjust the pH.

### Growing Mediums
*   **Option A (Full Hydro):** **Pumice (Bimsstein)** as the main medium + **Rockwool (Steinwolle)** for the seed zone.
*   **Option B (Hybrid):** **Pumice (Bimsstein)** as the main medium + a small amount of **Soil (Erde)** on top for the seed zone.

---

## 2. Technical Setup (The Wick System)

Proper wick placement is the "engine" of this system.

1.  **Wick Installation:**
    *   Place **one wick into each of the four feet** of the plant pot.
    *   Ensure the wicks are long enough to reach the very bottom of the reservoir.
2.  **Braiding & Weaving:**
    *   As you fill the pot with **Pumice (Bimsstein)**, weave/braid the wicks upwards through the medium.
    *   Do not just leave them at the bottom; they should reach high up into the pot to ensure even moisture distribution.
3.  **The Seed Zone (Top Layer):**
    *   Fill the pot with Pumice until about 2-3 cm from the top.
    *   In the center, place your **pre-soaked Rockwool cube** or a small amount of **Soil**.
    *   **Crucial:** Position the upper ends of the wicks **directly around** the Rockwool or Soil area where the seed will sit. This ensures the young seedling gets moisture immediately.

---

## 3. The 12/12 One-Bud Timeplan

### Phase 0: Germination
*   **Rockwool Prep:** If using Rockwool, soak it for 24h in pH-adjusted water (pH 5.5) before planting.
*   **Sowing:** Plant the seed and keep the area moist using a pipette (approx. 3ml water twice a day) until the seedling stands upright.
*   **Light:** Set your timer to a **12/12 cycle** (12h Light / 12h Dark) from Day 1.

### Week 1-2: Establishment
*   **Action:** Continue to keep the seed zone moist using the pipette.
*   **Development:** The seedling will stand up and start developing roots. Once the roots hit the wicks/pumice, the system becomes self-sustaining.

### Week 3: Activating the Reservoir
*   **Action:** Once the plant is established, fill the reservoir with your prepared solution.
*   **The Test:** If the water level in the reservoir drops over 48 hours, the capillary action is working. **Stop all top-watering now.**

### Week 4-5: Pre-Flowering & Stretch
*   **Action:** Ensure the reservoir never runs dry. The plant will stretch rapidly as it prepares to bloom.
*   **Nutrients:** Start using the full nutrient solution recipe as soon as the first white pistils appear.

### Week 6 to Harvest: Flowering Phase
*   **Action:** Maintain the reservoir with the nutrient solution.
*   **Observation:** If leaves turn very dark green or show "clawing," reduce the fertilizer dosage.

### Final 10 Days: Flushing
*   **Action:** Stop all fertilizer. Fill the reservoir with **pure, pH-adjusted water only**.
*   **Goal:** This forces the plant to use its remaining reserves, improving the final taste and smoothness.

---

## Critical Rules for Success
1.  **NO Top-Watering:** Once the wick system is active, **never water from above.** This prevents flushing accumulated salts from the surface into the roots, which causes immediate "nutrient shock."
2.  **pH is King:** In a pumice setup, the plant cannot buffer pH changes. Check your solution regularly.
3.  **Use the Pipette!** Guessing dosages will lead to unstable conditions and plant stress.
